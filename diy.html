Project 4: Getting Moldy

This project is a continuation of Project 3, where we used our 3D printed part to be our mold master. Again, this is a keychain that is meant to feature snap-off guitar picks. This project was actually a little bit more challenging for me because of the thinness of the part I made.

Initial Attempt

I initially tried to keep the parting line along on of the major faces of my part. The result was very poor imprint of the part in the silicone mold, and I had even warped my part. So I had to back and do a small redesign and change my strategy.

New Design and Draft Analysis

The new design features a really thin wall for the initial holes, and a new parting line should allow the part to be created with a 2 part mold. The one little "cheat" is the fact that leveraging the fact that the mold is silicone, I can keep those indents without fear that the mold will keep the part in.

Project 6: Final Fun (Final Project)

My final project is an attempt at making a module I could mount on my glasses to take photos from my point of view. Here are a few answers to questions that were proposed at the prompt of the question:

  1. What are you going to make?
    I intend on making a camera that is mountable to my glasses that I wear. The idea is similar to the snapchat spectacles, except that I don’t need a whole other pair of glasses.
  2. Why are you making it?
    All too often I feel like there are moments that one hopes to capture on camera but by the time you try to pull out your phone the moment has passed. In addition, sometimes when one is driving it could be unsafe to pull out your phone to do so. Therefore if there’s a camera that’s already poised to take pictures at the push of a button would be really great. Though I won’t be able to capture those moments that have already passed, it would make me happy to capture the moments yet to come.
  3. Who are you making it for?
    I’m making this for myself.
  4. How are you going to make it?
    I intend on mainly assembling the electronics from some off the shelf components, namely a Raspberry Pi Zero W, and a camera designed for it, and finally potentially a small custom PCB with some buttons. As for the mechanical components, I will be likely making the enclosure and glasses-mounting-device with 3D printed parts. In addition, I would like to make custom buttons perhaps out of molding techniques as well. Finally I would like to use LED’s on the device and it could make a lot of sense to use light pipes cut from acrylic to make them. It’s possible in addition to make molds of these parts too, or perhaps use other techniques not yet covered in class.
